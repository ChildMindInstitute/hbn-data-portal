Children's Perception of Interparental Conflict (CPIC)¶

Guided by Grych and Fincham's theoretical framework for investigating the relation between interparental conflict and child adjustment, The Children's Perception of Interparental Conflict Scale (CPIC) was developed to assess children's views of several aspects of marital conflict.

Domains Assessed: Distress Tolerance, Trauma

Note: The CPIC is administered to participants ages 8-18.

References: Grych, J.H., Seid, M. , & Fincham, F.D. (1992). Assessing marital conflict from the child's perspective: The Children's Perception of Interparental Conflict Scale. Child Development, 63, 558-572.

Time to Complete: 10 minutes
