Social Aptitudes Scale (SAS)Â¶

The SAS is a 10-item parent report scale that assesses a child behaves in different social situations compared to other children of the same age. Items are rated from above to below average.

Domains Assessed: Social Interaction

Note: The SAS is administered to parents of all participants.

References: Liddle, Elizabeth B., Martin J. Batty, and Robert Goodman. "The social aptitudes scale: an initial validation." Social psychiatry and psychiatric epidemiology 44.6 (2009): 508-513.

Time to Complete: 5 minutes
