The Consensus Diagnosis for participants is a set of diagnostic results based on the results of the K-SADS interview, based on DSM-IV criteria. After the K-SADS is administered to both a participant and his/her parent or caregiver, a research clinician determines which set of diagnoses are consistent between the child and parent/caregiver interviews.

Note:A consensus diagnosis is collected for all participants.
