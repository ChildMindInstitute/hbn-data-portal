Wechsler Intelligence Scale for Children-V (WISC-V)¶

The WISC-V is a measure of cognitive function in children and adolescents. Participants will complete the 10 core subtests: similarities, vocabulary, blocks, matrix, figure weights, digit span, coding, symbol search, visual puzzles, and pictures span.

Domains Assessed: Cognitive and Executive Functioning, Short Term Memory

Note: The WISC-V is administered to participants ages 6-17.

References: Wechsler, D. (2014). The Wechsler intelligence scale for children—fifth edition. San Antonio, TX: The Psychological Corporation.

Time to Complete: 60 minutes
